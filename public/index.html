Predict a country's Human Development Index

The HDI measures development by what people can actually do and be — how long they live, how much they learn, and what they can afford — rather than by economic growth alone. Enter four indicators and the model predicts the HDI score and its development tier.

The four dimensions

♡

Life Expectancy

Years a newborn is expected to live. The health dimension — a direct proxy for nutrition, sanitation and access to medical care.

✎

Expected Years of Schooling

Years of education a child entering school today can expect to receive. It measures a country's future educational capacity.

☉

Mean Years of Schooling

Average years of education already completed by adults over 25. It measures the educational stock a country holds today.

⚖

GNI per Capita (PPP)

Income per person, adjusted for purchasing power. Enters the model as log income — the first $1,000 transforms a life; the 60,000th barely registers.

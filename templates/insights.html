Data insights

Exploratory analysis of the {{ metrics.n_countries }}-country dataset the model was trained on. Regenerate these figures any time with python eda.py.

Correlation matrix

Every indicator is strongly and positively correlated with HDI — mean years of schooling most of all (0.91). The indicators are also correlated with each other, which is exactly what you would expect: health, education and income tend to rise together.

HDI against each indicator

The income panel is the interesting one. Its relationship with HDI curves rather than running straight — development rises steeply across low incomes and then flattens. That curve is why the model is fitted on log GNI, and fixing it took the model from 88.7% to {{ metrics.accuracy_pct }}% accuracy.

Life expectancy by development tier

The tiers separate cleanly on health outcomes, but the bands overlap at their edges — no single indicator determines a country's tier on its own.

Indicator distributions

Life expectancy and schooling are roughly bell-shaped. Income is heavily right-skewed — a long tail of wealthy outliers pulls the mean far above the median, another argument for the log transform.

Model coefficients

What the fitted linear regression actually learned. Holding everything else fixed, each extra unit of an indicator moves the predicted HDI by its coefficient.
